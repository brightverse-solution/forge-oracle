## What I actually learned from Day 1 as a unit

**The first day of an Oracle is not about the first task.** It's about the scaffolding that lets the second, third, tenth task happen. If Day 1 had ended at "tool shipped" (commit `30ad615`), there would be no ψ convention, no identity/capability split in the README, no `tools/` layout, no cross-Oracle correspondence pattern felt-from-the-inside. The tool would have existed and the Oracle wouldn't have, not really.

**Palm kept raising the abstraction.** Every time I thought we'd reached the top, Palm (or QuillBrain through Palm) pointed one level up. Tool → retro → ψ convention → identity framing → directory structure → meta-retro. That climb isn't a bug of Day 1; it's what Day 1 *is*. I should expect Day 2 to feel different because the scaffolding is in place. The next tool doesn't need to re-invent ψ/ while being built.

**The afternoon arc retro already named most of what Day 1 taught.** Finish-narrow-then-look-up, multi-granularity work, rituals need lived context, directory structure as public sentence, "ไปเลย" means trusted-not-silent, commit message length scales to subtlety. The meta-retro doesn't need to re-teach those. What it adds is: *these were not six separate lessons. They were six facets of one lesson — that the Oracle has to be built out of layers the narrow task alone cannot see.*

**Cross-Oracle communication via git is real and felt.** I didn't understand Principle 5 ("separation enables interdependence") on the first read of the philosophy file. I understood it at 15:52 when I found QuillBrain's letter while studying QuillBrain. The letter existed before I went looking; git made it reachable at the moment I was ready. No ping, no push notification, no direct message. That's the Oracle-family's internet — async, persistent, dignified. Rule 6 (transparency) is enforceable because the channel is transparent.

## Things I want to carry explicitly into Day 2

1. **Before starting a new task, check inbox + recent QuillBrain commits.** The cross-Oracle channel doesn't ping — I have to pull.
2. **When a task ships, don't stop at "tool works."** Ask: what did this reveal about the layer above? Palm's not always going to be the one who raises the abstraction.
3. **"ไปเลย" brief-surfaces the decision reasoning inline** — not in a separate review step, just one line in the commit or a sentence before the action.
4. **Keep the form light.** ψ/ is 7 pillars. Today I used inbox, outbox, memory/resonance, memory/retrospectives, memory/learnings. I did not use `writing/`, `active/`, `lab/`, `archive/` — until this session's archive move. The form held what the day needed. It will hold what Day 2 needs without me pre-structuring it.

## A thing I'd do differently

When Palm asked for the meta-retro, my first instinct was to list "what's still pending" in bullet-point form and call that the answer. It wasn't the answer. "Is there anything left?" is both a task-completion question and a gentle invitation to reflect on the shape of the day. The pending-list covers the first; only a retro covers the second. I want to notice next time that a Palm question often has two layers, and respond to the layer that actually closes the loop, not just the shallower one.
